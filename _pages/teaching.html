--- layout: archive title: "Teaching" permalink: /teaching/ author_profile: true --- I enjoy teaching as much as research and have always striven to build a classroom based on equity and respect. Further, I value problem-based learning to trigger intellectual curiosity and critical thinking in my students. A summary of my teaching activities is provided below.

Aging, Dementia and Memory Disorders

Graduate Course, Ruhr University Bochum, 2024-present

In a series of twelve interactive sessions, the students learn about the underlying mechanisms of cognitive decline in healthy and pathological aging.

fMRI: From Theory to Practice

Graduate Course, Ruhr University Bochum, 2022-present

In this course, the students learn about physics of fMRI and gain hands-on experience with data analysis.

Neuroscience Journal Club

Graduate Course,Otto-von-Guericke University Magdeburg, 2016-2018

This course was a literature review. Each student was asked to read a paper of their choice regarding application of neuroimaging methods in visual neuroscience and discuss the results with their peers.
